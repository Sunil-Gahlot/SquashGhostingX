⚡ Court Systems
6-Point & 10-Point

Two systems.
Infinite combinations.

6-point covers the four corners, mid-court drive, and T. It’s the foundation — builds the movement patterns that every squash rally demands. Master it and your court coverage transforms.

10-point adds bridging positions between the standard six. Longer arcs, more complex sequences, greater aerobic demand. Every setup takes under 30 seconds: pick system, set duration, choose intensity — and go.

Court systems
Court Configurations

Two systems. Every level covered.

Both use the same wooden court your training is measured on. Choose your system based on your level — and progress when you’re ready.

6-Point System

Six positions cover the four corners and both mid-court zones. The foundation of every ghosting programme — master the pattern and your court coverage fundamentally changes. Perfect for players building the movement habit from scratch.

1–6 court positions T — recovery point

10-Point System

Four bridging positions added between the standard six — creating longer movement arcs, more complex sequences, and greater aerobic demand. For players who’ve built the base and want to push their movement to match-winning levels.

1–10 court positions T — recovery point

Every great squash player shares the same secret. Thousands of hours alone on a court — sprinting to corners that hold no ball, playing strokes against empty walls. Ghosting. The most brutal, most effective solo training in the sport.

And for decades, players did it with nothing but a stopwatch and a hunch. No feedback on their zone balance. No data on whether forehand recovery was slower than backhand. No structure beyond “keep going.” The training was real — but the intelligence wasn’t there.

Squash GhostingX changes that. It gives every solo session a voice, a brain, and a memory. And because the best training shouldn’t come with a price tag, it’s completely free — for every player, at every level, forever.
