Your Phone Is the Best Squash Coach You've Never Had

6 min · Mobile App

I know how that sounds. Bear with me.

Private squash coaching is expensive, anywhere from $60 to $150 an hour depending on where you live, if you can even find a good coach with availability. Club coaching is cheaper but usually means group sessions where you get ten minutes of focused attention if you're lucky. Training three times a week gets costly fast, and most club players simply can't sustain it.

What a Mobile App Actually Does Well

A phone can't watch your technique or tell you your swing is too open; that still needs a real coach in the room. But what it does consistently, for free, any time you have court access: calls your ghosting positions, times your intervals, adapts to your skill level, tracks your court coverage over weeks, and enforces T-recovery on every single rep. For the movement and conditioning side of the game, a well-built app covers most of it.

The Offline Factor

Courts don't usually have WiFi. The changing room definitely doesn't. Squash GhostingX works entirely offline, everything runs on your device with no connection needed. Open the app, set your session, earphones in, and go. It doesn't need to check in with a server before letting you train.

17 Languages

This matters more than it seems. If English isn't your first language, processing coaching cues in English costs you something in real time, a small lag as your brain translates. Getting coached in Hindi, Arabic, French, Spanish, or another of the 17 supported languages is just cleaner. Faster to process, more natural to respond to. None of this replaces a skilled coach for technique work. But for movement, fitness, and court patterns on your own? Your phone is genuinely useful.
